Printable Electronics

About the course:

Printable electronic device development process which includes device designing, material and process selection, process optimization and finally device characterization is now in top attraction among electronic device manufacturing company and institute due to its various advantages like flexibility, easy to fabricate, less expensive, vide application area. It is assumed to be future of electronic devices and this course will serve as first step for those who have interest in the field of printable and printable electronics. The participants will be able to know future path in printable /wearable electronics including current research activities, worldwide.

Course Objectives:

  • The objective of the program is to train and develop professionals in the field of Printable electronics.
  • Prepare resource persons for guiding and motivating young students for taking printable electronics as a career.
  • Expose the participating faculty and students to comprehensive printable electronic design, process development and equip them to participate in related activities like functional ink formulation, device designing, printing and characterization.
  • Equip them with requisite skills, knowledge and competence for effective implementation of printable electronic devices, which includes supercapacitors, OLED, RFID tags, sensors and electrodes etc.

Outcomes:

  • Equipping teachers with skills and knowledge so as to improve the teaching learning process and arouse interest in the subject of Printable Electronics.
  • Exchanging of Ideas to bring the focus on the relevant issues.
  • Making specific recommendation for future course of action to promote efficient & effective trends and technologies in Printable Electronics

Target audience:

  • Faculty of engineering colleges.
  • Ph.D scholars pursuing research in Electrical/Electronics
  • M.Tech/B.Tech students doing thesis/projects in Electrical/Electronics.
  • ECE/EEE Faculty of Government and Private engineering colleges/universities.
  • Engineers from Wireless Industry and R&D Institutions.
